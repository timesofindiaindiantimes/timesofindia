Maharashtra Govt Orders Major Bureaucratic Reshuffle: 21 IPS, Multiple IAS Officers Transferred

TNN
Updated: Aug 20, 2025 10:48 IST

CHENNAI: A lawyer has moved the Madras high court challenging the decision of the Tamil Nadu govt in appointing four senior IAS officers as spokespersons of the state govt.

The petition, filed on Monday, seeks to quash the appointment of the officers on grounds of procedural irregularities and alleged violation of service rules. The petitioner told the court that the appointments bypassed required clearances and consultations with the concerned departments.

According to the petition, the appointments were made hastily and affected the transparency expected in public postings. Counsel for the petitioner argued that spokesperson roles are pivotal and should be filled through a clear and accountable process.

Government counsel, in response, argued that the appointments were made under the discretionary powers vested in the state administration and followed the internal guidelines. The court has listed the matter for preliminary hearing next week.

What petitioner claims

The petitioner has sought a declaration that the appointment process was void for being arbitrary and contrary to established norms. He has also sought interim relief to stay the functioning of the appointed officers as spokespersons until the court passes further orders.
